What social implications does biometrics have? Any political or social concerns? Any innate harms or risks?

There are many social implications and concerns associated with biometric technology. One of the main concerns is privacy. Collecting and storing unique biometric information, such as facial characteristics or fingerprints, poses serious risks if unauthorized access is made. As biometric databases grow, the risk of misuse and identity theft increases. This raises questions about the adequacy of security measures to safeguard sensitive personal information. Biometric algorithms, particularly in facial recognition, raise issues of bias. Ethical issues arise when biometric algorithms display inaccurate or discriminatory patterns, which can harm individuals and communities.

How can we look at the biometrics and its applications from different ethical lenses?

Viewing biometrics through different ethical perspectives reveals different social implications. From a practical point of view, the advantages of increased security and convenience may outweigh the disadvantages, making biometrics a valid option. On the other hand, from an ethical point of view, individual rights and privacy considerations may lead to skepticism about the widespread adoption of biometrics. By viewing biometrics through diverse ethical perspectives, one can gain a better understanding of its social implications, allowing for more nuanced discussions and informed decisions.

What are your future recommendations for using biometrics in a less harmful way? Are any technological or social strategies possible? Any policy-level recommendations?

There are a number of recommendations and strategies that can be used to reduce the risks with biometrics. Firstly, strong regulations should be in place for collection, storage and use of biometrics. These would focus on consent, data security and transparency. Secondly, technological innovation should focus on developing safer algorithms in facial recognition to reduce ethical concerns. Thirdly, social awareness can be utilized to help inform the public about the advantages and disadvantages of biometrics which would inform and spread awareness. Lastly, collaborating with the government and technology developers will help create ethical standards to be able to use biometric technology ethically.
